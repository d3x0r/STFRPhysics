The framework curve is generated from the constrained occupation profile: r1/r2 fit the leading rise, r3 sets the start of the long near-flat support shelf, r4 sits near the visible edge, and r5 is the final cutoff at or just beyond the sampled galaxy edge. The pale dashed line is the independently scaled Newtonian rotation curve obtained if the occupation profile is treated as an ordinary enclosed-mass profile.

The pale dashed Newton curve treats the same occupation profile as an ordinary enclosed-mass proxy and fits only its amplitude; this is a comparison curve, not the framework-gradient result. The radii are structurally constrained: r1/r2 control the leading rise, r3 is kept near the center to define the start of the broad flat shelf, r4 is kept near the visible-edge shelf, and r5 is allowed to reach or slightly exceed the brightness edge while remaining beyond r4. Rho is an occupied-support envelope, not brightness itself.
